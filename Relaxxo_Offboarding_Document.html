01 / Four A note as we close

Closing the chapter.

As our engagement on Relaxxo comes to a close, this document gathers — in one place — a clear record of the nine months of work, the results it produced, the pricing decisions that shaped the back half of the year, and the state of the account being handed back.

When we took over PPC management in mid-September 2025, the scalp massager was on a declining trajectory. Advertising efficiency had broken down — ACOS was running above 100% and TACOS sat near 80%, meaning the account was spending roughly as much to advertise as it was earning. The product was priced at $5.29, daily orders averaged around 20, and the first weeks under our management were deliberately steady while we audited and rebuilt the foundation.

Nine months later, the account is in a fundamentally different position. It reached a monthly peak of $12,822 in December 2025 — nearly five times the $2,593 it did in December 2024 — absorbed a price increase from $5.29 to $7.49 across the engagement, and rebuilt advertising efficiency from an unsustainable starting point into a controlled, profitable operating range. The work moved the listing from a loss-making ad account into a genuine, scalable asset.

02 / Four What we built together

The work, in summary.

Beyond the headline numbers, the engagement followed a clear arc: stabilize, then scale, then raise price and protect margin. Each phase was tied to a goal the Relaxxo team set, and each goal was met. This is how the account moved.

i. Sales trajectory across the engagement

Monthly Total Sales — Sep 2025 to Jun 2026

ii. Goals set, goals met

The engagement was driven by a sequence of targets the Relaxxo team set, each one raising the bar as the previous was cleared.

  • Reach 40+ orders per day. The first month was deliberately steady at ~20 orders/day while we rebuilt the account. From mid-October, momentum shifted decisively — daily orders climbed to 50–60, comfortably clearing the target and driving the Q4 scale-up.
  • Bring TACOS down to 50%, then under 30%. From a starting point near 80%, disciplined spend and compounding organic rank brought TACOS to a low of 41% in December at peak volume — the strongest efficiency-at-scale month of the engagement.
  • Raise price without losing volume. On our recommendation, price was lifted in small increments from $5.59 to $6.99. The gradual approach worked: the listing absorbed each step while sales continued to climb, validating real pricing power in the listing.

iii. Two deliberate phases

Q4 2025 — Scale into peak

We leaned into the holiday window, widening targeting and increasing bids on high-converting placements. The result was the $12,822 December peak — the largest month in the account's history and nearly 5× the prior December — at a TACOS of just 41%.

Q1 2026 — Efficiency hold

Q1 delivered roughly $30.7K in sales — 283% above Q1 2025. TACOS rose to ~60% early in the quarter as planned, then we pulled spend back in February and March to bring it to 47%, holding a positive trajectory into spring.

iv. Key optimizations delivered

  • Efficiency reset at takeover. Inherited an account running above 100% ACOS; rebuilt the campaign structure and eliminated wasted spend to establish a sustainable baseline.
  • Order-velocity scale-up. Took daily orders from ~20 to 50–60, the volume foundation that made the Q4 record possible.
  • Incremental price strategy. Guided pricing from $5.29 to $6.99 in measured steps — a +32% increase the listing absorbed without losing momentum, lifting margin on every unit.
  • Year-over-year step change. December +395% and Q1 +283% versus the prior year — the clearest evidence that the work changed the account's level, not just its month-to-month noise.

03 / Four A candid note on Q2

The price move, and what followed.

A closing document is only useful if it is honest about the hard parts as well as the wins. The second quarter saw the account soften, and the cause is clear enough that it is worth stating plainly — so the team can plan around it.

i. What happened in March

In mid-March, the team requested a price increase to $7.99 — which would have placed Relaxxo near the highest price point in the category. We advised against the full move and instead agreed on a smaller step to $7.49. Even at the moderated level, this proved to be the turning point for the quarter.

The listing began to lose search rankings, and sales volume declined with them. Monthly sales moved from $11,820 in February to $6,242 in March and $4,567 in April as the higher price compressed conversion and organic rank slipped on key terms.

ii. The trade-off we made to defend velocity

At the team's request, we intensified advertising to defend sales velocity at the new price point. That effort worked — sales recovered to $6,070 in May and $6,828 in June — but it came at the cost of efficiency. TACOS rose to roughly 67%, and the majority of sales are now being driven by paid advertising rather than organic search. This was a deliberate, agreed trade-off: volume held, but on a more expensive footing than the account ran at its Q4–Q1 best.

The honest read

The $7.49 price is defensible on margin per unit, but at current organic rank it requires heavier ad support to hold volume. The lever that most directly improves Q2's efficiency is price: a modest step back toward the $6.99 level — where the listing demonstrably scaled — would likely relieve the pressure on paid spend and let organic rank recover.

iii. What we'd watch from here

  • Organic rank on the top 10 keywords. The leading indicator of where total sales settle. Track weekly — recovery here precedes any sustainable reduction in ad spend.
  • Price elasticity around $6.99–$7.49. The data already shows the listing scaled cleanly through $6.99. Testing back toward that band would clarify how much of the Q2 softness is price-driven.
  • TACOS trend, not just the level. A TACOS of 67% is acceptable briefly to defend rank; left unmanaged it erodes the margin the price increase was meant to capture.
  • Session volume. If sessions recover while conversion holds, rank is returning; if sessions stay depressed, the price ceiling is the binding constraint.

04 / Four Opportunity & handover

What's next, and a clean handover.

The account is being returned in a far stronger position than it was found in, with a clear growth lever already sitting in the catalog. Below is the opportunity ahead, followed by the state of the account at handover.

i. Two new products, not yet launched

Relaxxo has introduced two new products into the catalog. PPC campaigns have not yet been launched for either. Both show strong market potential and represent the most direct opportunity to grow overall revenue — and to do so at better advertising efficiency than the core listing currently runs at, since fresh launches can be built on the campaign structure, negative-keyword discipline, and bidding playbook already proven on the scalp massager.

Why this matters now

The core listing is mature and price-constrained in Q2. New products are the cleanest path to incremental revenue without leaning harder on the existing listing's paid spend — and they inherit nine months of category learning from day one rather than starting cold.
